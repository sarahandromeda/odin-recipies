Indian Red Lentil Dal by The Wanderlust Kitchen

Description

Lentil Dal is an Indian style dish that is usually made vegetarian or vegan. Dal can be made with any kind of lentils and tweaked to give a variety of flavor profiles.

This recipe is for a dal made from Red Lentils which are shelled green lentils. During cooking, some of the lentils will smash to give the dish a thick porridge-like consistency. The cost of most of the needed ingredients is relatively low making this a great less-expensive but very filling option.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 3 cups room temp water
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds (whole)
  • 1 (2 inch) cinnamon stick
  • 3 cardamom pods
  • 1 cup diced yellow onion
  • 1 green chili pepper, minced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp finely minced ginger root
  • 1/2 tsp ground tumeric
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt
  • 1 medium tomato, diced
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Chopped cilantro

Steps

  1. Add 1 cup of rinsed lentils and 3 cups of water to pot. Set temperature to medium and cook for 20 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a skilled on medium heat. Add whole cumin seeds, cinnamon, and cardamom pods. Cook for 60-90 seconds, until fragrant.
  3. Add onion, chili pepper, garlic, and ginger. Cook for 4-5 minutes, until onions are translucent.
  4. Add ground tumeric, cardamom, paprika, salt, and tomato to fry pan with spices. Cook until tomato begins to fall apart, 2-3 minutes. Discard cardamom pods and cinnamon stick.
  5. When lentils are done, drain water only off the top. Leave some water in lentils to help make the soup. Stir fry pan mixture into lentils.
  6. Add lemon juice, stir well. Taste and adjust as necessary. If soup is too thick, add water or boil off if too wet.
  7. Serve with basmati rice and cilantro garnish.
